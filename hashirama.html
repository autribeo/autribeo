Hashirama Senju


1 Wood Dragon Jutsu (木龍の術 - Mokuryū no Jutsu)"


Definition:

  • Hashirama creates a massive wooden dragon from chakra to attack or bind his opponents.

How it works:

  • The wooden dragon moves dynamically, chasing and wrapping around its target.

  • It can absorb chakra from the opponent, including immense sources like Tailed Beasts.

Significance:

  • This technique is both offensive and suppressive, symbolizing Hashirama’s unparalleled ability to dominate opponents through chakra control.

2 Wood Human Jutsu (木人の術 - Mokujin no Jutsu)


Definition:

  • Hashirama summons a colossal wooden humanoid figure, roughly equal in size to or larger than a Tailed Beast.

How it works:

  • The Wood Human can engage directly in combat with entities like Tailed Beasts or a Perfect Susanoo, using its massive fists for devastating attacks.

  • It often works in tandem with the Wood Dragon, amplifying its sealing and combat abilities.

Significance:

  • This technique showcases Hashirama’s dominance on the battlefield, as it combines both raw power and strategic adaptability.

3 True Several Thousand Hands (真数千手 - Shinsuusenju)


Definition:

  • Hashirama summons an enormous statue of a Thousand-Armed Buddha, one of his ultimate techniques.

How it works:

  • The statue’s thousands of hands can deliver an overwhelming barrage of punches.

  • Its size and power are enough to overpower even Madara’s Perfect Susanoo.

Significance:

  • This technique symbolizes Hashirama’s unmatched strength and spiritual connection to nature, embodying peace and balance through sheer power.
